Vibration Decay Rate Analysis After a ball strike the hurley vibrates at its natural resonant frequency. The rate at which this 
vibration dampens follows an exponential decay curve A(t) = A₀ × e^(−λt). The decay rate constant lambda (λ) and its derived half-life 
describe how quickly the vibration dies out after impact. 

 

A high lambda and short half-life indicate the energy transferred cleanly into the ball characteristic of a sweet spot hit. 
A low lambda and long half-life indicate the hurley retained vibration energy  characteristic of an off center strike. 

Two recording sessions with similar peak G values (~21–23G) produced dramatically different decay rates lambda 130–149 (half-life 4–5ms) 
versus lambda 12–16 (half-life 42–56ms). This confirms that peak G alone is insufficient to characterize strike quality and that decay rate 
is an independent metric capturing contact quality.
