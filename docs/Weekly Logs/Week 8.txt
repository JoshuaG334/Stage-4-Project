Week 8 


This week I focused on the power system design for the Smart Hurley, researching battery options, charging circuitry, and protection requirements for a safe and reliable embedded system. 

 

Battery Selection 

 

To determine the most suitable power source for the Smart Hurley, I evaluated several common battery types coin cells, AA/AAA NiMH packs, Li-ion cylindrical cells, and single-cell Li-Po batteries. The decision was based on four criteria: 

 

1. Required current draw (IMU + piezo + microphone + BLE microcontroller) 
2. Physical size and weight, since everything must fit inside the hurley handle 
3. Rechargeability and ease of integrating a charging module 
4. Ability to handle peak loads, especially during Bluetooth transmission 

 

Power Requirements 

 

The ESP32 can draw 80–250 mA during BLE transmissions  while the sensors add another 10–30 mA depending on sampling rate. 

https://blog.usro.net/2025/04/understanding-esp32-power-consumption-full-breakdown/?utm_source 

 
This means the system needs a battery that can comfortably supply 120–300 mA bursts without voltage collapse. 

 

Why the other battery options didn’t work 

 

Coin Cells 

They only provide 3V, which is too low for the ESP32. 

They cannot supply the high current bursts required for BLE they are limited to 20 mA. 

Voltage collapses instantly under load device resets. 

Non-rechargeable 

 

 

 AA/AAA 

A 3-cell pack gives only 3.6V, which drops to 3.0V under load borderline for ESP32 

A 4-cell pack gives 4.8V requiring step-down regulation more heat & space 

Physically too large and heavy for inside a hurley 

 

Li-ion Cylindrical Cells 

18650 cells are far too large for the hurley handle 

 

 

Why the 3.7V Li-Po battery was the best fit 

 

Li-Po voltage ranges 3.0–4.2V ideal for an ESP32 board with onboard regulators. 

No boost/step-down converter required simpler more efficient power system. 

Easily supplies 300–500 mA more than enough for BLE peaks. 

Flat pouch-style cells fit naturally in the hurley handle. 

Low weight avoids affecting balance or swing feel. 

Works perfectly with the TP4056 charger, allowing safe USB charging. 

Built-in overcharge / over-discharge protection is common on Li-Po packs.
