Week 6

Week ending 24/10/2025 

 

This week I finalized the core sensor's for the Smart Hurley and explored smart sports equipment in other fields to benchmark features and validate design choices. I also evaluated microcontroller options and began outlining the system architecture. 

 

Sensors: 

Motion Tracking: Selected the BMI160 IMU for 6-axis motion sensing accelerometer + gyroscope, ideal for capturing swing speed, rotation, and movement patterns. 

-Library's and tutorials online using the sensor on the Arduino IDE 

 

Impact Detection: Chose the Murata 7BB-20-6L0 piezoelectric sensor to detect strike events and measure impact force. 

-Small Piezo, No buzzer  

 

Wireless Communication: Decided to use a microcontroller with built-in Bluetooth, eliminating the need for a separate Bluetooth module. The specific board (ESP32 DevKit V1, FireBeetle C6) is still under evaluation. 

 

Exploration of Similar Equipment 

To benchmark the Smart Hurley concept I researched smart sports equipment in tennis which has seen significant innovation in motion and impact tracking: 

 

Babolat Play and Zepp Tennis Sensor use IMUs accelerometers + gyroscopes to track swing speed, stroke type, and racket orientation. This is directly comparable to my use of the BMI160 which offers similar motion tracking capabilities tailored to hurling. 

 

These tennis systems also include impact detection algorithms, often based on vibration analysis. My project uses a piezo sensor for this purpose, which provides a physical signal when the hurley strikes a sliotar or surface.
