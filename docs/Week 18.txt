Week 18

Inspection of the Arduino BMI270 library revealed the accelerometer and gyroscope Output Data Rate (ODR) were hardcoded as: 

 

sens_cfg[0].cfg.acc.odr = BMI2_ACC_ODR_100HZ; 

sens_cfg[1].cfg.gyr.odr = BMI2_GYR_ODR_100HZ; 

 

This confirmed that the default IMU sampling rate was limited to approximately 100 Hz 

 

ODR Increased to 400 Hz 

Both accelerometer and gyroscope ODR were modified to: 

BMI2_ACC_ODR_400HZ 

BMI2_GYR_ODR_400HZ 

 

Measured effective sampling rate: 

~150 Hz 

Although the internal ODR was increased to 400 Hz, the delivered sampling rate did not reach 400 Hz. 

 

ODR Increased to 800 Hz 

Both sensors were then configured to: 

BMI2_ACC_ODR_800HZ 

BMI2_GYR_ODR_800HZ 

 

Measured effective sampling rate: 150Hz 

 

Increasing ODR beyond 400 Hz did not increase effective output rate. 

Attempts to configure higher than 800 Hz resulted in configuration errors 

 

I2C Clock Speed Increased 

The I2C clock was increased to 1 MHz using: Wire.setClock(1000000); 

 

This was only accelerometer 

Measured effective sampling rate: 155 Hz
